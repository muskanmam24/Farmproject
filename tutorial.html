Crop Cultivation Guide

Staple Crops (Rice, Wheat, Corn)

Planting Time

Rice: June-July (Kharif season)

Wheat: November-December (Rabi season)

Corn: May-June or September-October

Optimal Conditions

Soil pH: 5.5-7.0

Water: 1000-1500mm rainfall

Temperature: 20-30Â°C

Fertilization

NPK ratio: 4:2:1 for most cereals

Organic compost: 5-10 tons per hectare

Top dressing: Urea at tillering stage

Common Pests

Rice: Stem borers, leaf folders

Wheat: Aphids, rust

Corn: Armyworms, borers

Fruit Trees & Plantations

Common Fruit Trees

Mango: Plant in rainy season

Citrus: Requires well-drained soil

Apple: Needs winter chilling

Banana: Plant suckers in pits

Maintenance

Pruning: Annual during dormancy

Irrigation: Drip recommended

Fertilization: Balanced NPK + micronutrients

Mulching: Conserves moisture

Harvesting

Maturity signs: Color change, easy separation

Time: Cool morning hours

Tools: Secateurs, picking poles

Post-harvest: Quick cooling preserves quality

Vegetable Cultivation

Seasonal Vegetables

Tomatoes: 6-8 hours sunlight

Leafy greens: Partial shade preferred

Root crops: Loose, well-drained soil

Cucurbits: Warm soil for germination

Planting Guide

Spacing: Avoid overcrowding

Succession planting: Stagger plantings

Companion plants: Marigolds deter pests

Rotation: Prevent disease buildup

Harvesting

Morning harvest: Retains moisture

Tools: Sharp knives/shears

Frequency: Regular picking promotes yield

Storage: Cool, humid conditions

Cash Crops (Cotton, Sugarcane, Tea)

Planting Requirements

Cotton: Warm climate, well-drained soil

Sugarcane: Tropical/subtropical regions

Tea: Acidic soil, high rainfall

Coffee: Shade-grown at elevations

Market Considerations

Quality standards: Meet buyer requirements

Contract farming: Secure prices in advance

Processing: Adds value to raw products

Export potential: Certification needed

Pest & Disease Management

Sucking Pests

Aphids: Use neem oil spray (2%) weekly

Whiteflies: Yellow sticky traps + neem extract

Thrips: Blue sticky traps, spinosad

Chewing Pests

Bollworms: Release Trichogramma wasps

Locusts: Early morning spraying with malathion

Caterpillars: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis)

Soil Pests

Cutworms: Collars around seedlings

Nematodes: Marigold intercropping

Grubs: Neem cake application

Fungal Diseases

Powdery mildew: Baking soda spray

Rust: Remove infected leaves

Blight: Copper-based fungicides

Bacterial Diseases

Blight: Copper sprays, resistant varieties

Wilt: Soil solarization

Canker: Prune infected branches

Viral Diseases

Mosaic viruses: Control aphid vectors

Leaf curl: Remove infected plants

Yellowing: Use certified disease-free seeds

Natural Pesticide Recipes

Garlic-Chili Spray

Mix 100g garlic + 50g chili in 1L water, strain after 24 hours

Use: Broad-spectrum repellent

Neem Oil Spray

5ml neem oil + 2ml liquid soap in 1L water

Use: Disrupts insect life cycle

Fermented Compost Tea

Aerate compost in water for 48 hours, strain and spray

Use: Boosts plant immunity

Soil Health Management

Soil Testing

When: Before each cropping season

Parameters: pH, N-P-K, organic matter

Sampling: Take from multiple spots

Improving Soil Fertility

Organic matter: Compost, manure

Green manure: Grow and plow under legumes

Biofertilizers: Rhizobium, Azotobacter

Soil Conservation

Contour farming: On slopes

Mulching: Reduces erosion

Cover crops: Protect bare soil
